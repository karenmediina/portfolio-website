Eye Disease Diagnosis using Deep Learning

Eye diseases can potentially cause blindness in patients. Here I used convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and vision transformers to identify diseases from fundus photos, specifically glaucoma, cataracts and diabetic retinopathy. The CNN outperformed the vision transformer’s performance, most likely due to the size of the data set, with an accuracy of 95%

Machine Learning to Intensive Care Patients

During the pandemic, hospitals in my area were reaching maximum capacity before being able to provide care for all patients. I wanted to determine which type of patient runs more risk of needing an intensive care unit based on their characteristics, using decision trees, random forest and XGBoost. This way, by retraining the models constantly, we can get an accurate and up to date of who’s being affected the most.
